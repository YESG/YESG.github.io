Project HOPE

Provides Healthcare, Opportunity, Provisions, and Education to those in need in Bhavnagar, India.

Healthcare

YES-Good works with the P.N.R Society Hospital to subsidize the costs of surgeries and healthcare equipment for the patient. Healthcare costs for hearing aids, cataract surgeries, artificial legs for polio victims, and rubella vaccinations have been dramatically lowered in order for disabled persons in the area to recieve the quality healthcare they require.

Opportunity

We believe that everybody should have the opportunity to earn a livelihood and be a productive member of society. We are kick starting a small craft store for the goods created by students at the Cerebral Palsy vocational school and connect them with customers across the globe. We have already provided capital, like a cart and a scale, to disabled adults who now own their own local successful small businesses. Our aim is to provide the opportunity for everybody to live meaningful lives.

Provisions

We help provide about 150 people (a Leprosy Colony, cancer patients, and dialysis patients) with a steady supply of food provisions every month. The care package contains 5 kilograms of wheat (to make a bread called roti), 2 kilograms of rice, and 500 grams of dal. Each package only costs about 200 rupees (about $3.50) and can feed one person for fifteen days.

Education

Because we believe education is an integral part of modern society, we have been supporting the K.L Institute for the Deaf, which currently provides education for 350 hearing impaired students from K through 12th grade in Western India. We also aim to support their future plans to expand to the university level and hope to serve as liasons between them and Gallaudet University (which provides undergraduate and graduate education degrees to hearing impaired students in America). We also support computer and technology education for the disadvantaged in Bhavnagar through the AT&T Technology Park, which provides I.T Training and computer access for the disabled.
