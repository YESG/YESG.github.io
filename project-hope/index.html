Project HOPE

Provides Health, Opportunity, Provisions, and Education to those in need in Bhavnagar, India.

Healthcare

YES-Good works with the P.N.R Society Hospital to subsidize the costs of surgeries and healthcare equipment for the patient. Healthcare costs for hearing aids, cataract surgeries, artificial legs for polio victims, and rubella vaccinations have been dramatically lowered in order for disabled persons in the area to recieve the quality healthcare they require.

Opportunity

We believe that everybody should have the opportunity to earn a livelihood and be a productive member of society. We are kick starting a small craft store for the goods created by students at the Cerebral Palsy vocational school and connect them with customers across the globe. We have already provided capital to disabled adults who now own their own local successful small businesses. We want to provide the opportunity for everybody to live meaningful lives.

Provisions

What started in 2000 as a movement to provide supplies to a Leprosy Colony has blossomed to provide a support system to provide food to those who need it. Today, we help provide about 150 people (from cancer and dialysis patients all the way to the original Leprosy Colony) with a monthly ration of food provisions. The care package (which only costs about 200 rupees, or $3.50, per person for 15 days) contains 5 kilograms of wheat (to make a bread called roti), 2 kilograms of rice, and 500 grams of dal.

Education

Education is an integral part of modern society and thus we support public and free education for everybody. Therefore we have been supporting the K.L Institute for the Deaf which currently teaches K-12 students. We are also assisting them in their plans to expand to the university level and hope to serve as liasons between the K.L Institute for the Deaf (an Indian school that currently only offers K-12 education) and Gallaudet University (which provides undergraduate and graduate education degrees in America).
